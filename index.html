Research Interests

I am deeply interested in system-level environmental perception and monitoring, driven by my experience as an RA at Duke University, where I developed a fault-assessment framework to evaluate the safety and reliability of autonomous vehicle systems. My work with sensor data and perception algorithms has inspired me to explore:

AI-driven environmental perception: advancing robust, real-time scene understanding across diverse domains (not limited to automotive).
Autonomous vehicle safety: optimizing safety-critical decision-making processes for autonomous vehicles.
Connected vehicle communication: designing architectures for reliable, low-latency data exchange between sensors, vehicles, and infrastructure.
Large-scale perception systems: integrating sensor fusion, data analytics, and cloud-based services to build end-to-end monitoring pipelines.

Additionally, I maintain a strong interest in cloud computing and energy systems, particularly:

Data processing and mining: extracting insights from industrial and environmental datasets.
Computer vision & NLP: applying deep learning to automated inspection, fault diagnosis, and human-machine interaction.
Robotics and control: developing intelligent automation workflows for resilient, scalable operations.
